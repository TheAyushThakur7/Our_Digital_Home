My Divu

I've been thinking about everything you've said, and for the first time I'm trying to read it without immediately trying to prove that I've changed or convince you to stay.

A Genuine Apology

I'm sorry. Not just for the things I said, but for making you feel like your words weren't truly reaching me. You kept telling me how you felt, what hurt you, what was missing, and instead of fully understanding your pain, I kept trying to defend myself or explain why things would be different. Looking back, I understand how exhausting that must have been for you.

"You deserved someone who listened first and spoke later. Too many times I did the opposite."

I'm also sorry for the moments where I was insensitive or disrespectful. Some of the things I said came from panic, frustration, or fear of losing you, but none of that makes them okay. You shouldn't have had to carry the weight of my reactions.

Understanding • Healing • Gratitude
Your Gift of Love

You once chose to love me with your whole heart. That wasn't something small. It was a gift. And if my actions slowly made you lose faith in us, then I understand why you're hurting today.

I don't want this apology to be another attempt to argue that I've changed. Whether you believe I've changed or not is your decision, and I respect that. I just want you to know that I genuinely recognize the pain I caused, and I'm deeply sorry for it.

Thank you for every conversation, every smile, every time you cared about me, checked on me, worried about me, and loved me in ways I probably didn't appreciate enough at the time. Those moments will always mean something to me.

Moving Forward

If, one day, you ever decide to give me another chance, I don't want to earn it through promises. I would want to earn it through consistent actions. And if that day never comes, I'll still be grateful that our paths crossed because you taught me lessons I'll carry for the rest of my life.

"You once became my safest place, and I'm sorry if I failed to become yours."

No matter what happens, I sincerely wish you peace, happiness, and a life where you always feel understood, valued, and loved the way you deserve.

I'm truly sorry, Divu.
